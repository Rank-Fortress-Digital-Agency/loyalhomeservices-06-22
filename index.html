CHECKING AND CLEAN YOUR AIR FILTERS REGULARLY.

Check and clean your air filters regularly. A clogged filter will reduce the airflow through your system, which will cause the unit to work harder and result in higher energy bills. In addition, a dirty filter will also reduce the system’s effectiveness, meaning that it won’t be able to cool or heat your home as efficiently as it should.

If you have pets, it’s also important to regularly vacuum and shampoo your carpets, as they can collect pet hair and dander. It will help improve the air quality in your home and make it easier for your HVAC system to do its job.

In addition to regular maintenance, there are a few other things that you can do to improve the air quality in your home. These include:

– Investing in an air purifier: An air purifier can help remove airborne contaminants from the air, making it cleaner and healthier to breathe.

– Adding plants to your home: Plants help filter harmful toxins from the air, so adding a few to your home can make a big difference.

– Avoiding toxic cleaning products: Many household cleaning products contain harmful chemicals that can pollute the air. Whenever possible, opt for natural and non-toxic alternatives.
